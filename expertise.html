Carbon Markets & Climate Finance

Verra / VCS Gold Standard GHG Protocol SBTi Article 6.2 CDM ITMOs

Carbon markets are not a single system — they are a patchwork of competing methodologies, jurisdictional rules, and evolving international frameworks that demand fluency at multiple levels simultaneously. I work across all of them.

At the project level, this means understanding verification standards (Verra, Gold Standard, CDM), the mechanics of additionality and baseline assessment, and how project design documents translate scientific claims into tradable credits. At the regulatory level in Kenya, this means knowing the Climate Change (Carbon Markets) Regulations 2024 in granular detail — land classification rules, benefit-sharing schedules, NEMA fee structures, and how Article 6.2 bilateral authorization changes the financial calculus for every project seeking international buyers.

I am UNFCCC RINGO-accredited with active exposure to Article 6 negotiations and NDC framework consultations, and I have built public-use tools that make Kenya's carbon market regulations navigable for developers and investors.

ESG Disclosure & Advisory

IFRS S1/S2 TCFD CDP ISSB GRI Standards IFC Performance Standards

ESG disclosure is where corporate sustainability commitments meet legal obligation — and the gap between them is where most institutions currently sit. My advisory work is designed to close that gap practically, not ceremonially.

I am a certified practitioner across IFRS S1/S2, TCFD, CDP, ISSB, and GRI Standards — the full stack of frameworks that financial institutions and listed companies in Kenya need to understand as the 2027 compliance deadline approaches. I have built ESMS implementation frameworks aligned with IFC Performance Standards for active client engagements, and developed ESG integration roadmaps that take organizations from baseline carbon inventory to measurable reduction targets.

What distinguishes my approach is that I have delivered this across multiple sectors simultaneously — banking, manufacturing, agriculture — and across three East African regulatory environments, which means the frameworks I produce are built to travel.

Data, Analytics & Visualization

R Programming GIS / Spatial Analysis Data Visualization MRV Frameworks M&E Design

Environmental analysis is only as useful as its accessibility. The core skill here is not technical fluency for its own sake — it is the ability to take raw, complex environmental data and translate it into something that a CFO, a development finance institution, or a policy team can act on.

My technical toolkit includes R programming for statistical analysis, GIS and spatial mapping for conservation and land use work, and data visualization for national-scale climate data. I have designed M&E frameworks for conservation programmes operating across arid and semi-arid landscapes, built national GHG emissions dashboards from raw inventory data, and produced quantitative analysis that informed executive-level governance strategy at the UN.

The throughline is MRV — measurement, reporting, and verification — as applied analytical infrastructure. Building systems that make environmental performance legible, auditable, and useful is what connects my field data work to my advisory work.

Multilateral Engagement & Governance

UNFCCC Processes UN Major Groups Member State Liaison Civil Society Coordination UNEA Processes

Global environmental governance looks different from the inside. Working at UNEP's Nairobi headquarters means understanding how 193 national interests become, or fail to become, a collective position — and how civil society organizations fit into that process, or don't.

My experience facilitating between UN Major Groups and Member State representatives gave me a working understanding of how intergovernmental processes actually function: where consensus is built informally, how regional group dynamics shape outcomes, and what it takes to produce analysis that influences decisions made in rooms where the stakes are explicitly global.

This background informs how I approach client advisory — particularly for institutions that need to navigate the relationship between national regulatory requirements, regional frameworks, and international standards. The ability to read governance systems, not just comply with them, is a specific and useful skill.
